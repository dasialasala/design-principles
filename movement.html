Definition (by the book)

"The flow of lines can move the eye across the page and screen. Lines, then, can create movement, and different kinds of linear movement tend to communicate different kinds of symbolic messages" (57-58).

Definition (in my words)

Movement is used to bring the illusion of action into a still photo. Movement goes hand-in-hand with rhythm and patterns. It helps the eyes follow a map of where to look at a photo and when. It helps the viewer to avoid skipping ahead in a photo which can cloud their judgment of what the photo means to them.

Real World Connection

For me, movement often implies the feeling of chaos. In everyday life, constant movement makes it hard to focus which keeps the eyes at work in terms of trying to figure out what element to follow at any given moment. On the contrary, movement also implies grown and development.
